Giniloh Money Flow Simulator

The **Frictionless Money Flow Simulator** is an interactive financial modeling playground designed to help you visualize, configure, and backtest automated cash flow pipelines. 

By simulating daily transactions and monthly interest cycles, this tool shows how a forward-looking "waterfall" routing system can automatically optimize your net worth. It replaces stressful manual monthly budgeting with a zero-friction system that deposits income, fills emergency reserves, wipes out high-interest debt, maximizes tax-advantaged accounts, and channels leftover surpluses into long-term wealth generators—all automatically.

What is a Frictionless Simulator? The concept of a "Frictionless Simulator" is rooted in the Unified Loh-Friction Philosophy, an architectural framework designed to eliminate the cognitive, emotional, and structural barriers that cause people to fail at wealth building
. Traditional budgeting is a retrospective chore that forces users to categorize past expenses, inducing guilt and decision fatigue
.
The frictionless approach flips this into prospective expense management
. By planning with intention and creating a pre-determined allocation of resources, money is automatically routed to its highest-utility destination—whether that is paying off debt, capturing 401(k) matches, or investing—the moment it enters your financial ecosystem
. It eliminates the willpower needed to save and protects you from behavioral mistakes (like panic-selling during a crash) and structural friction (like login fatigue, which is bypassed using modern FIDO biometric standards)
.
How the Giniloh.com Simulator Works The Giniloh Frictionless Money Flow Simulator is an interactive modeling playground that brings this theoretical orchestrator to life
. Under the hood, the engine simulates daily transactions, monthly interest cycles, and market returns to help you backtest automated cash flow pipelines
.
The core of the system relies on dual-threshold account mechanics (Overbalance and Underbalance rules)
:
Overbalance Sweeps: If your primary checking account exceeds a custom "Ceiling" threshold (T 
over
​
 ), the excess surplus is instantly swept down an algorithmic waterfall, targeting emergency funds, high-interest debt, and tax-advantaged accounts first, before hitting a taxable brokerage
.
Underbalance Pulls: If an unexpected expense drops your checking account below your "Floor" safety threshold (T 
under
​
 ), the system automatically pulls funds from your High-Yield Savings Account (HYSA) to prevent an overdraft
.
To ensure real-world fidelity, the simulator enforces strict regulatory safeguards, including blocking outbound transfers from Roth IRAs (preventing IRS penalties), enforcing 6-day ACH settlement holds, and restricting daily trades for accounts under $25,000 to mimic Pattern Day Trader (PDT) limits
.
How to Use the Simulator You manage the simulator through three primary dashboards:
System Flow Control (Top): Control the clock speed (run daily, fast-forward, or step manually) and select macroeconomic backtesting regimes (e.g., Baseline growth, Stagflation, or a 2008-style Market Contraction)
.
Interactive Money Flow Canvas (Middle): A visual map of your accounts. You can click nodes (like Checking or HYSA) to open a parameter drawer and manually drag sliders to set your current balances, ceilings, and floors
.
AI Orchestration Chat & CLI (Bottom): You can configure pipelines using natural language. Because the simulator needs exact math to function, your prompts must include numerical values
. For example, instead of saying "save money," you must say "Sweep 500 from checking to savings" or "Set checking ceiling to 5000"

## 1. Why Automated Cash Flows Matter in Real Life

Traditional budgeting is a **retrospective chore**. It asks you to categorize past expenses, feel guilty about overspending, and manually transfer funds between accounts every month. This creates cognitive load and decision fatigue, often resulting in money sitting idle in low-interest checking accounts or failing to reach investment goals.

The **Loh-Friction Philosophy** turns budgeting on its head:
* **Forward-Looking Allocations**: Money is routed the moment it enters your ecosystem. 
* **Zero Decision Friction**: By configuring rules once, you eliminate the emotional willpower needed to save and invest.
* **Optimized Compounding**: Cash is never left dragging; it immediately goes to its highest-utility destination (paying off 18% debt, securing free 401(k) matches, or earning yield in index funds).
* **Automatic Safety Nets**: If an unexpected expense drains your checking account, savings are pulled back automatically to protect you from overdrafts.

Spending 15 minutes in this simulator allows you to stress-test these rules under historic market regimes (steady growth, stagflation, or a 2008-style crash) before configuring them in your real-life bank and brokerage accounts.

## 2. Detailed Account Node Directory

The canvas models eight distinct accounts. Every account has specific logic rules, limits, and time-frame behaviors.

| Account ID | Display Name | Account Type | Real-Life Purpose & Mechanics |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| `checking` | **Primary Checking** | Hub | **The entry point.** All income deposits here. When the balance exceeds its **Ceiling ($T_{over}$)**, surplus cash sweeps to the waterfall. If the balance drops below its **Floor ($T_{under}$)**, it triggers an emergency pull from HYSA. |
| `hysa` | **HYSA (Emergency Fund)** | Cash / Yield | **Safety reserve.** Earns interest (APY) computed daily and accrued every **30 simulated days**. Sweeps fill it up to its ceiling (typically 3–6 months of expenses), after which it is bypassed. |
| `match401k` | **Employer 401k Match** | Equity | **Free money.** Captures pre-tax contributions up to the employer matching cap (annual YTD limit). Balances grow/shrink daily based on simulated stock market index returns. |
| `debt` | **High-Interest Debt** | Liability | **Credit cards / high-rate loans.** Charges interest (APY) accrued every **30 simulated days**. The waterfall routes cash aggressively to pay this balance down to $0 before passing to standard investments. |
| `hsa` | **Pre-tax HSA** | Health / Equity | **Triple-tax-advantaged account.** Tax-deductible contributions, tax-free growth, and tax-free withdrawals for healthcare. Sweeps are capped by the annual IRS YTD limit. |
| `ira` | **Roth IRA** | Retirement | **Post-tax tax-free growth.** Sweeps are capped by the annual IRS YTD limit. Standard rules apply: **outbound sweeps are strictly blocked** to simulate early-withdrawal tax penalties. |
| `max401k` | **Workplace 401k Max** | Retirement | **Pre-tax voluntary max-out.** Routes cash after securing matching funds, paying high-rate debt, and filling IRAs, capped at the annual IRS YTD limit. |
| `brokerage` | **Taxable Brokerage** | Taxable Equity | **Surplus compounder.** Receives the absolute overflow with infinite capacity. Subject to daily stock market fluctuations. Used for money needed before retirement age. |

### Units of Time & Rates in the Simulator
* **1 Day**: The fundamental step unit. Daily income is added and market returns are compounded every business day.
* **30 Days**: The billing/statement cycle. Every 30 days, interest is calculated and posted (positive dividends to `hysa`, negative interest charges to `debt`).
* **Annual Limits**: IRS caps (e.g., $7,000 for `ira`, $4,150 for `hsa`) reset when the simulation restarts. YTD trackers prevent exceeding these limits.

## 3. Regulatory Safeguards & Simulation Rules

To model real-life compliance and protect users from destructive financial behaviors, the engine enforces six built-in guardrails:

1. **Circular Dependency Protection**
   * *The Rule*: You cannot route funds in an infinite loop (e.g., Checking $\rightarrow$ HYSA and HYSA $\rightarrow$ Checking).
   * *Simulation response*: Commands creating cyclic paths are blocked with a `Circular dependency detected` error.
2. **IRA Outbound Protection**
   * *The Rule*: Siphoning cash out of a Roth IRA or 401(k) for daily expenses triggers high IRS tax penalties.
   * *Simulation response*: Automated sweeps out of `ira` or 401(k) accounts are blocked immediately.
3. **Realistic Transfer Latency Holds**
   * *The Rule*: Cash transfers do not settle instantly. Standard transfers take time, ACH deposits have hold periods, and account transfers (ACATS) freeze assets.
   * *Simulation response*: Transfers are held in a pending state:
     * Standard Transfers: **1-day hold** (`T+1`).
     * Checking $\rightarrow$ Brokerage: **6-day ACH settlement hold**.
     * Brokerage $\rightarrow$ Checking: **15-day ACAT settlement lock**.
4. **Pattern Day Trader (PDT) Limits**
   * *The Rule*: Equity accounts with balances below $25,000 are restricted from excessive daily trading.
   * *Simulation response*: If total equity across `brokerage`, `ira`, and `401k` is under $25,000, you are limited to a maximum of **2 sweeps to equity accounts per day**. Further sweeps are blocked.
5. **Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Lookback**
   * *The Rule*: Moving money rapidly back and forth between accounts mimics layering schemes flaggable by compliance.
   * *Simulation response*: Within a **60-day window** of receiving a transfer, an account can only route funds *back to the originating account*. Sweeps to new third-party accounts are blocked.
6. **Ardal Loh-Gronager Behavioral Safeguard Overlay**
   * *The Rule*: Panic-selling during market crashes locks in temporary paper losses as permanent realized losses.
   * *Simulation response*: In the **Market Contraction (Crash)** scenario, the simulator automatically pauses on **Day 18** (when the market drops over 10%). The interface locks until you complete the 6-question emotional centering checklist.

## 5. Orchestrator CLI Command Syntax Reference

In the Frictionless Moneyflow Simulator, the Natural Language / LLM configuration chat operates as an intelligent translation layer. It bridges everyday speech (e.g., "sweep 500 from checking to emergency fund") and the simulator's strict internal syntax (e.g., `checking [500] hysa`).

Here is a detailed breakdown of how the parser translates user requests, what commands are generated under the hood, and how users should format their instructions.

### 1. The Core Translation Mechanism
The parser in `llmParser.ts` runs incoming text through a two-tier parsing workflow:
* **Local Heuristics (Regex)**: It first attempts to match commands using fast, local regex matching. For example:
  * Set parameters: matches `set [account] [field] (to) [value]` $\rightarrow$ `set checking ceiling 6000`
  * Sweep commands: matches expressions starting with send, sweep, route, transfer, or move, followed by an amount and the source/target account $\rightarrow$ `checking [500] brokerage`
* **LLM API Fallback (Gemini or DeepSeek)**: If the local regex does not match, the simulator leverages a cloud LLM (DeepSeek's `deepseek-chat` using `PUBLIC_DEEPSEEK_API_KEY` or Gemini's `gemini-2.5-flash` using `PUBLIC_GEMINI_API_KEY` depending on the environment keys configured). The system instructions prompt the LLM to act as a strict API translation layer returning:
  * A valid simulator command on the first line.
  * A friendly explanation on the second line.

### 2. Supported Account Identifiers & Aliases
The parser contains mapping dictionaries to translate natural language synonyms to actual simulator node IDs:

| System Node ID | Common User Aliases (Input) |
| :--- | :--- |
| `checking` | checking, primary checking, checking account |
| `hysa` | hysa, emergency fund, savings, savings account, high yield savings |
| `match401k` | match401k, 401k match, employer match |
| `debt` | debt, credit card, high-interest debt, loan |
| `hsa` | hsa, health savings |
| `ira` | ira, roth ira |
| `max401k` | max401k, 401k max, workplace 401k |
| `brokerage` | brokerage, taxable brokerage, investments, stocks |

### 3. "Can I just say 'add automatic savings to my savings account'?"
No, not exactly—you need to specify the numerical value or limits.

Because the underlying Giniloh simulator must know how much money to route or what ceiling/floor limits to set, conversational prompts **MUST INCLUDE NUMERIC VALUES**.

* **Why vague prompts fail**:
  If a user inputs: *"add automatic savings to my savings account"*
  * *Local Regex Parser*: Fails to match because it expects a specific amount (like $500 or 10%).
  * *LLM API Fallback*: Will struggle because the prompt lacks the amount of the sweep. The system prompt directs the LLM to translate strictly to `Source [Amount] Target`. Without an amount, it cannot formulate a valid command and will fallback to a warning/help state.
* **Recommended way to instruct**:
  Users should specify the amount/percentage and the source/destination accounts. Here are ideal examples:
  * To build cash flow pipelines:
    * *"Sweep 500 from checking to savings"* $\rightarrow$ Translates to: `checking [500] hysa`
    * *"Route 10 from checking to stocks"* $\rightarrow$ Translates to: `checking [10] brokerage` (percentages are represented without % in the bracket)
    * *"Send 2000 from checking to roth ira"* $\rightarrow$ Translates to: `checking [2000] ira`
  * To set account thresholds:
    * *"Set checking ceiling to 5000"* $\rightarrow$ Translates to: `set checking ceiling 5000`
    * *"Set debt balance to 8000"* $\rightarrow$ Translates to: `set debt balance 8000`
    * *"Set emergency fund threshold to 15000"* $\rightarrow$ Translates to: `set hysa ceiling 15000`

### 4. Basic Command Table

| Command Pattern | Action | Real-Life Application Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| `set [node] [balance/ceiling/floor] [value]` | Configures account thresholds or balances. | `set checking ceiling 6000` <br> `set debt balance 5000` |
| `[Source] [[Amount]] [Target]` | Establishes an automated routing pipeline of `$Amount` (if fixed) or `%Amount` (if percentage). | `checking [1500] hysa` <br> `checking [10] match401k` |
| `reset` | Reverts all account stats, history, and storage configuration to defaults. | `reset` |
| `clear` | Clears the command console history. | `clear` |

## 7. Real-Life Personal Money Flow Practice Scenarios

Here are 4 real-life scenarios formatted with execution steps (including both AI commands and manual settings), and the expected user observations.

### Scenario 1: Aggressive Debt Paydown vs. Earning Interest
* **Scenario**: A user takes home $2,800 every two weeks ($200/day). They currently have $3,000 in checking but want to cap it at $2,000. They also have $4,000 in high-interest credit card debt and want to watch the system automate payoff.
* **Execution**:
  * *AI Chat*: User types: *"Set my income to 200 a day. Set my checking balance to 3000 and limit it to 2000. Set my debt to 4000."*
  * *Manual alternative*: Click checking node $\rightarrow$ set balance to 3,000 and ceiling to 2,000. Click debt node $\rightarrow$ set balance to 4,000.
* **Observation**: When starting the clock, the $1,000 surplus sweeps to HYSA and then cascades to pay down High-Interest Debt. The user watches the debt decrease to $0 while seeing interest charges accrue monthly until fully cleared.

### Scenario 2: The Emergency Expense (Floor Protection)
* **Scenario**: A user has $15,000 in HYSA. They want to maintain a checking floor of $1,500. An unexpected expense drops their checking balance to $500. They want to verify floor restoration rules.
* **Execution**:
  * *AI Chat*: User types: *"Set my checking floor to 1500. Set checking balance to 500. Set my HYSA to 15000."*
  * *Manual alternative*: Select Checking node $\rightarrow$ drag Floor to $1,500 and Balance to $500. Select HYSA $\rightarrow$ set balance to $15,000.
* **Observation**: Step 1 day. The system detects the checking deficit and triggers an automated "Underbalance sweep" of $1,000 from HYSA back to checking to restore the floor.

### Scenario 3: The Income Surge and Waterfall Overflow
* **Scenario**: A user receives a promotion ($500/day). The HYSA is full ($15,000) and debt is zero. They want to verify long-term investment cascading.
* **Execution**:
  * *AI Chat*: User types: *"Set my income to 500 a day. Set my HYSA balance to 15000 and its ceiling to 15000. Set my debt to 0."*
  * *Manual alternative*: Set income/day to $500, set HYSA balance and ceiling to $15,000, and ensure Debt is zero.
* **Observation**: Run at 5x. Daily surpluses bypass HYSA and debt, cascading down to max out Employer 401(k) Match, fill pre-tax HSA, fill Roth IRA, and finally overflow into the Taxable Brokerage.

### Scenario 4: Testing the System Guardrails (Prohibited Transfers)
* **Scenario**: A user attempts to draw funds out of their Roth IRA back to Checking to cover daily expenses.
* **Execution**:
  * *AI Chat*: User types: *"Route 1000 from my IRA to checking."* (Translates to: `ira [1000] checking`)
* **Observation**: No cyan path is drawn. The CLI and Audit Log print: `"ERROR: Automated sweep from IRA blocked to prevent early distribution penalties"`, enforcing real-world retirement account guidelines.
