Career Velocity Calculator: 4 Scenario Walkthroughs
This guide outlines 4 realistic career scenarios. Follow the step-by-step instructions in the calculator to see how different parameters affect long-term wealth compounding, effective hourly rates, and transition friction.

Scenario 1: The Early-Career Optimizer (Aggressive Salary Jumps)
Objective
Examine the impact of strategic job-switching in sub-$100k wage bands, where talent demand is highly elastic. We compare a loyal "Stayer" receiving standard merit increases against a "Switcher" who hops every 2 years for a 25% increase.

Step-by-Step Configuration
Open the Core Parameters tab:
Starting Base Salary: $65,000
Combined Tax Rate: 25%
Portfolio Return Rate: 8%
Annual Bonus: 5%
Employer Match Limit: 3%
Deferral Contribution: 6%
Open the Stayer Settings tab:
Annual Merit Raise: 4.1% (representative internal pool cap)
Cost of Living Index: 1.0
Expected Weekly Hours: 40
Paid Time Off (PTO): 15
Open the Switcher Details tab:
Hop Interval: 2 Years
Hop Salary Increase: 25%
Non-Hop Annual Raise: 3.0%
New Hire Signing Bonus: $3,000 (gross)
COBRA Transition Cost: $1,200
Unvested Match Loss: 100% (cliff match loss)
Unvested Bonus Loss: 50%
Cost of Living Index: 1.0 (maintaining same location)
Expected Weekly Hours: 40
Paid Time Off (PTO): 15
Trajectory Insights
Year 10 Salary Divergence: By Year 10, the Switcher's base salary compounds to over $155,000, compared to the Stayer's $96,000 (a 60%+ premium).
Compounded Raise Delta Portfolio: Even after subtracting COBRA costs, match forfeitures, and bonus timing cuts, the switcher accumulates over $92,000 in invested wealth by Year 10 just from routing their raise differentials into the index portfolio.


Scenario 2: The Golden Handcuff Dilemma (Match Forfeiture vs. Pay Raise)
Objective
A mid-career engineer earning $120,000 gets a recruiter outreach offering a 12% raise. However, they are currently 2 years into a 3-year 401(k) match vesting cliff. This walkthrough calculates whether the raise compensates for losing unvested matching assets.

Step-by-Step Configuration
Open the Core Parameters tab:
Starting Base Salary: $120,000
Combined Tax Rate: 32%
Portfolio Return Rate: 8%
Annual Bonus: 10%
Employer Match Limit: 6% (generous matching program)
Open the Stayer Settings tab:
Annual Merit Raise: 4.1%
Open the Switcher Details tab:
Hop Interval: 3 Years (timing the jump at the cliff end)
Hop Salary Increase: 12%
Non-Hop Annual Raise: 3.0%
New Hire Signing Bonus: $5,000 (used to offset matching losses)
COBRA Transition Cost: $1,200
Unvested Match Loss: 100% (forfeiting the matches accumulated during the 2 years before the transition)
Unvested Bonus Loss: 50%
Trajectory Insights
The "Switching Deficit": Hovering over Year 3 shows that the Switcher Net Income dips below the Stayer Net Income (creating a negative Delta of ~-$4,000). This is due to forfeiting over $21,000 in cumulative unvested employer matches.
Negotiation Levers: The user can see that a $5,000 signing bonus is insufficient. They must negotiate a signing bonus of at least $15,000 to break even on Year 3 transition friction.
Scenario 3: The Work-Life Balance Tradeoff (Effective Hourly Rate)
Objective
Compare a high-paying startup switcher role requiring 55-hour weeks and minimal vacation time against a stable stayer corporate role with standard 40-hour weeks and generous leave.

Step-by-Step Configuration
Open the Core Parameters tab:
Starting Base Salary: $130,000
Combined Tax Rate: 35%
Open the Stayer Settings tab (Stable Corporate):
Annual Merit Raise: 4.1%
Expected Weekly Hours: 40
Paid Time Off (PTO): 25 days (generous leave)
Open the Switcher Details tab (High-pressure Startup):
Hop Interval: 3 Years
Hop Salary Increase: 25% (yielding a nominal jump to $162,500)
Expected Weekly Hours: 55 (startup overtime)
Paid Time Off (PTO): 10 days (limited vacation)
Trajectory Insights
Effective Hourly Rate Divergence: Hovering over Year 4 reveals that despite the Switcher earning a much higher nominal base salary ($167,375 vs the Stayer's $146,654), the Stayer's Effective Hourly Rate is higher ($81.25/hr vs the Switcher's $68.50/hr).
Key Takeaway: When accounting for the extra 15 hours of work per week and 15 lost days of vacation, the switcher is taking a pay cut on a per-hour basis.
Scenario 4: Geographic Relocation Arbitrage
Objective
Analyze the wealth-generating potential of moving from a high Cost of Living (CoL) area (like San Francisco) to a lower CoL area (like Austin) while taking a slight nominal pay cut.

Step-by-Step Configuration
Open the Core Parameters tab:
Starting Base Salary: $150,000
Combined Tax Rate: 35%
Open the Stayer Settings tab (San Francisco):
Annual Merit Raise: 4.1%
Cost of Living Index: 1.0 (baseline)
Open the Switcher Details tab (Austin, TX):
Hop Interval: 3 Years
Hop Salary Increase: -5% (nominal pay cut to adjust for local market)
Cost of Living Index: 0.70 (30% lower housing and food expenses)
Trajectory Insights
Purchasing Power Normalization: Adjusting the Switcher CoL index to 0.70 increases their normalized take-home pay immediately.
Compounded Raise Delta Portfolio: By Year 10, the Switcher's compounded portfolio delta reaches over $115,000 in additional purchasing power, demonstrating that geographic arbitrage can override a nominal salary reduction.
